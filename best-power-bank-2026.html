Frequently Asked Questions

How many mAh do I need?

For phones, 10,000mAh gives you 2-3 full charges — that's the sweet spot for most people. For laptops, you'll want 20,000mAh or more with at least 65W output. If you're just looking for emergency backup, 5,000mAh is enough for one charge and fits in any pocket.

Can a power bank charge a laptop?

Yes, but it needs USB-C Power Delivery (PD) with at least 45W output. Most ultrabooks charge fine at 45-65W. Gaming laptops and MacBook Pros prefer 100W+. Check your laptop's charger wattage — your power bank needs to match or exceed it for full-speed charging. See our laptop vs phone comparison →

How long do power banks last?

Quality power banks (Anker, Baseus, Samsung) last 3-5 years or 500+ charge cycles before capacity noticeably degrades. Cheap no-name brands may only last 1-2 years. Store your power bank at 50% charge when not in use for maximum lifespan.

Is it safe to charge my phone overnight with a power bank?

Yes — quality power banks have overcharge protection that stops current flow when your device hits 100%. However, keeping any lithium battery at 100% for extended periods isn't ideal for long-term health. It won't damage anything, but charging to 80% is better for battery longevity.

Can I take a power bank on a plane?

Yes, with limits. FAA/TSA rules: power banks up to 100Wh (~27,000mAh) are allowed in carry-on luggage without approval. 100-160Wh requires airline approval. Over 160Wh is prohibited. Power banks are NEVER allowed in checked bags. Most power banks on this list are airline-approved except the Crave 50,000mAh.

What's the difference between PD, QC, and regular charging?

USB-C PD (Power Delivery) is the universal fast-charging standard — works with everything and supports up to 240W. Qualcomm Quick Charge (QC) is Android-specific and tops out at 100W. Regular USB charging is 5W-10W. For maximum compatibility, look for power banks with USB-C PD support.
