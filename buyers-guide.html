Power Bank Buyer's Guide

Cut through the marketing jargon. Here's what actually matters when choosing a portable charger.

The power bank market is flooded with options ranging from $10 to $200+. Most of the specs on the box are designed to confuse you into thinking you need the most expensive option. This guide tells you what actually matters and what's just marketing noise.

Understanding Capacity (mAh)

Milliamp-hours (mAh) measures how much energy a power bank stores. But here's the catch: you never get the full rated capacity in practice.

A 10,000mAh power bank delivers roughly 6,000-7,000mAh of usable charge due to energy lost as heat during voltage conversion. So when we say "2-3 phone charges," we're accounting for this real-world loss.

Capacity guide by use case:

  • 5,000mAh: Emergency backup. One phone charge. Fits in any pocket.
  • 10,000mAh: Daily carry. 2-3 phone charges. The most popular size.
  • 20,000mAh: Travel essential. 4-5 phone charges or one laptop charge.
  • 25,000-27,000mAh: Maximum airline-allowed. Multi-day trips without outlets.
  • 30,000-50,000mAh: Off-grid/van life. NOT allowed on planes.

Charging Speed: What the Watts Mean

Wattage determines how fast your devices charge. Here's what different speeds feel like in practice:

  • 5-10W: Slow. Your phone charges in 3-4 hours. Fine for overnight.
  • 18-22.5W: Fast. Phone charges in 1.5-2 hours. Good for most people.
  • 30-45W: Very fast. Phone in under an hour. Can slow-charge some laptops.
  • 65-100W: Laptop-speed. Charges ultrabooks at full speed.
  • 100-250W: Premium. Charges gaming laptops and multiple devices simultaneously.

Important: Your device must also support the charging speed. A power bank with 100W output won't charge an old iPhone any faster than a 20W one — the phone limits the input.

Charging Protocols Explained

Not all "fast charging" is the same. Here are the main protocols:

  • USB-C PD (Power Delivery): The universal standard. Works with iPhones, Android, laptops, tablets, Switch. This is what you want.
  • Qualcomm Quick Charge (QC): Android-focused. QC 3.0/4.0 is common. Good but less universal than PD.
  • Samsung Super Fast Charging: Samsung's proprietary 25-45W. Only works at full speed with Galaxy devices.
  • Apple MagSafe: Wireless magnetic charging for iPhone 12+. Convenient but slower than wired.

Our advice: Always prioritize USB-C PD. It works with everything and is future-proof. QC is a nice bonus for Android users.

Ports: How Many Do You Need?

More ports = more devices charging simultaneously. But there's a catch: total wattage is usually shared between ports. A "100W" power bank might only deliver 65W + 18W + 15W when all three ports are in use.

  • 1 USB-C port: Fine for single-device users. Simplest option.
  • 1 USB-C + 1 USB-A: The sweet spot. Covers modern and legacy devices.
  • 2 USB-C + 1 USB-A: Ideal for laptop + phone + earbuds.
  • 3+ ports: Family trips or charging multiple devices overnight.

Size and Weight Considerations

There's always a trade-off between capacity and portability:

  • Pocket-friendly (under 7 oz): 5,000-10,000mAh. Daily carry without noticing it.
  • Bag-friendly (7-14 oz): 10,000-20,000mAh. Fits in any backpack or purse.
  • Travel-sized (14-24 oz): 20,000-27,000mAh. Noticeable weight but worth it for trips.
  • Heavy (24+ oz): 30,000mAh+. Stays in your bag or at base camp.

If you're choosing between two similar options, pick the lighter one. You'll actually carry it more often.

Safety Features That Matter

Lithium batteries can be dangerous if poorly made. Look for these safety features:

  • Overcharge protection: Stops charging when your device is full
  • Over-discharge protection: Prevents the power bank from draining too low
  • Short circuit protection: Shuts off if a short is detected
  • Temperature monitoring: Reduces output if the battery gets too hot
  • UL/FCC certification: Third-party safety testing

Red flags: No-name brands on Amazon with suspiciously high capacity claims (like "50,000mAh" in a tiny package), missing certifications, or prices that seem too good to be true. Stick with established brands: Anker, Baseus, INIU, Samsung, Nitecore.

Airline Rules (Important for Travelers)

The FAA and TSA have strict rules about power banks on planes:

  • Under 100Wh (≈27,000mAh): Allowed in carry-on. No restrictions.
  • 100-160Wh: Allowed in carry-on with airline approval. Max 2 per person.
  • Over 160Wh: Prohibited on all flights.
  • Checked luggage: Power banks are NEVER allowed in checked bags.

To calculate Wh: multiply mAh × 3.7V ÷ 1000. Example: 26,800mAh × 3.7 ÷ 1000 = 99.16Wh (just under the limit — by design).

How to Make Your Power Bank Last Longer

  • Store at 50% charge when not using for extended periods
  • Avoid extreme temperatures — don't leave it in a hot car
  • Use the included cable or a quality certified cable
  • Don't fully drain it regularly — charge when it hits 20%
  • Keep it clean — dust in ports causes connection issues
